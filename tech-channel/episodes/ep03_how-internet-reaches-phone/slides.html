Idea 1 / 4 — The "wireless" myth, busted early

"WiFi" is only the last few metres.

📱 Phone ))) radio ))) 📡 Router
  • Your phone talks to your router using radio waves.
  • That wireless hop is tiny — just to the box on your wall.
  • After the router? No more "air".
Katixo KhojLab
Idea 2 / 4 — The internet is physical

After that, it's wires. Lots of them.

📡 Router→ 🏙️ City fibre→ 🌊 Undersea cable→ 🏢 Data centre
  • Fibre-optic cables run across cities and countries.
  • Giant cables on the ocean floor connect the continents.
  • ~99% of the internet is physical cable — not air, not satellites.

Idea 3 / 4 — Finding the right computer

Your data goes to one exact computer.

# you type a name…
youtube.com

# …which maps to an address (IP)
142.250.195.78

# → a specific server, in a building
  • Every server has an address — its IP.
  • Your request travels to that address…
  • …and the reply comes back to your phone.

Idea 4 / 4 — Why it feels instant

Milliseconds? It's light through glass.

Mumbai → London

~7,000 km
≈ 120 ms round trip

  • Fibre carries your data as pulses of light.
  • Light is the fastest thing in the universe.
  • So even across the planet, it feels instant.

Why it matters

Now buffering & lag make sense.

  • A server far away = more distance = more delay. That's ping / lag in games.
  • "The cloud" is just servers in big buildings, joined by these cables.
  • Cut one undersea cable, and a whole region can slow down.

Common myth — "yeh galat samajhte hain"
✗ Myth

"The internet is wireless — it floats through the air and satellites."

✓ Reality

~99% is physical cable, including undersea fibre between continents. Wireless (WiFi, mobile) is only the last hop to you.

Recap — 2 things to remember
  1. The internet is a giant web of physical cables — wireless is just the last few metres to you.
