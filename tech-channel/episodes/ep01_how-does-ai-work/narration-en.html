How does AI like ChatGPT actually work? You use it almost every day — but what's really happening inside? Today, let's open the box. No hype, no jargon.

02The Hook

You type "I am going ho…" and your phone guesses "home". That tiny guess? That is the entire secret behind ChatGPT — just scaled up a billion times. Stick around for five minutes and this "magic" will completely make sense.

03Today's question

So here is today's one question: how does it decide what to say next — and does it actually know things, or is it just guessing? Let's find out.

[CUE] Slow down. Let the question land before moving on.
04Idea 1 — Prediction machine

Idea number one: ChatGPT is a prediction machine. Give it "The cat sat on the blank", and it does not look up an answer in a database. It simply predicts the most likely next word. "Mat"? Very likely. "Banana"? Almost never. It picks the top one, adds it, and predicts again.

[CUE] Reveal the probability bars one by one as you say mat / floor / sofa / banana.
05Idea 2 — Trained on text

But how does it know "mat" is likely and "banana" isn't? Because it trained on a massive amount of text — billions of sentences from books, websites, conversations, and code. It did not memorise facts like a list. It absorbed the patterns of language — like a student who read millions of pages and just learned how sentences flow.

[CUE] Reveal the chips (books, websites, code) as you list them.
06Idea 3 — One token at a time

Here's the part that surprises people: it builds an answer one piece at a time. Each little piece is called a token. It predicts one token, adds it to the sentence, then feeds the whole thing back in to predict the next. "The… sky… looks… blue… because…" — one token at a time, incredibly fast.

[CUE] Reveal the token chain left-to-right; read it with a steady rhythm.
07Idea 4 — Context / attention

Now, how does it stay on topic? It weighs every word you wrote. If you say "I left my phone in the car, can you help me find it?" — it works out that "phone" and "car" matter most for the answer, not "the" or "my". This idea — paying attention to context — is literally what made modern AI work.

[CUE] Point to "phone" and "car" highlights as you say them.
08Why it matters

And that's the beautiful part. This one simple idea — predict the next token — scales up to almost anything. The same trick writes an essay, debugs code, drafts an email, answers a question. But never forget: it is predicting, not understanding.

[CUE] Land hard on "predicting, not understanding" — this sets up the myth.
09Common myth

Which brings us to the biggest myth. People think ChatGPT knows the truth and looks it up. It doesn't. It predicts the most likely-sounding words. So it can be completely confident — and completely wrong. We call that a hallucination. So always verify anything important.

[CUE] Reveal the green "Reality" card after a short beat on the myth.
10Recap

Quick recap. One: AI like ChatGPT is just next-word prediction, at a massive scale. Two: it doesn't think or know — it guesses patterns. So use it, but check the facts that matter.
