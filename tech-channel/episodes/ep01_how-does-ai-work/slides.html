Idea 1 / 4 — It's a prediction machine

It predicts the next word.

  • It doesn't look up an answer.
  • It picks the most likely next word…
  • …then repeats, again and again.

  • …not a saved list of facts it copies.

Idea 3 / 4 — How it builds an answer

One piece at a time.

# prompt
Why is the sky blue?

# it generates token by token, feeding each back in →
The      → The sky      → The sky looks
→ The sky looks blue   → The sky looks blue because …

Each word is a token. Pick one, add it, predict the next. Repeat.

Katixo KhojLab
Idea 4 / 4 — Why it stays on topic

It weighs every word you wrote.

"I left my phone in the car, can you help me find it?"
  • To choose the next word, it asks: which earlier words matter most?
  • Here, phone + car shape the answer — not "the" or "my".
  • This "pay attention to context" trick is what made modern AI work.

Why it matters

Same trick → essays, code, emails, answers.

  • One simple idea — predict the next token — scales to almost any text task.
  • That's why a chatbot can debug code and write a poem.
But remember

It's predicting, not understanding.

Common myth — "yeh galat samajhte hain"
✗ Myth

"ChatGPT knows the truth and looks it up."

✓ Reality

It predicts the most likely words. So it can sound confident and still be wrong — that's a hallucination. Always verify.

Recap — 2 things to remember
  1. AI like ChatGPT = next-word prediction, at massive scale.
  2. It doesn't think or know — it guesses patterns. So check the important facts.
