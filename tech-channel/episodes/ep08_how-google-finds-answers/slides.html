Idea 1 / 4 — It doesn't search the live web

It searches a pre-built index.

  • When you search, Google does not go scan the live internet.
  • It looks inside a giant pre-built index…
  • …like the index at the back of a textbook — which word is on which page.
Picture this

Word → list of pages it appears on.
Already built. Just look it up.

Katixo KhojLab
Idea 2 / 4 — Where the index comes from

Crawlers build it before you search.

  • Programs called crawlers (or "spiders") roam the web 24/7.
  • They visit pages, follow links, read the words…
  • …and note which word appears on which page.
🕷️ visit page
🔗 follow links
📖 read words
🗂️ add to index

All of this happens before you ever type a thing.

Idea 3 / 4 — Why the lookup is instant

Looking up the index is milliseconds.

time it takes
  • Scan whole web = impossibly slow.
  • Look up the index = a few milliseconds.
  • Just like finding a word via a book's index vs reading every page.

Idea 4 / 4 — The real magic

Finding is easy. Ranking is hard.

Millions of pages match your words. Which goes on top?
  • The real magic is ranking — deciding the order.
  • Signals: relevance · quality · links from other sites · freshness · your location.
  • Finding pages is easy; ranking them well is the hard problem.
Katixo KhojLab
Why it matters

It explains things you've noticed.

  • Brand-new pages take time to show up — they must be crawled first.
  • Results differ by person and place — location is a ranking signal.
  • SEO = helping your page rank, so it lands higher in the order.

Search isn't one trick — it's crawl, index, then rank.
