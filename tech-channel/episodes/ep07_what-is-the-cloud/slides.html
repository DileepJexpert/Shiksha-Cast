Today's Question
When something is "in the cloud",
where does it actually sit — and whose computer is it?
Katixo KhojLab
Idea 1 / 4 — It's just someone else's computers

"The cloud" = other people's computers.

  • These computers are called servers.
  • You use them over the internet, instead of your own device.
  • "In the cloud" = sitting on a company's server somewhere.
In short

There is no "cloud".
It's just computers — far away.

Katixo KhojLab
Idea 2 / 4 — Data centres are real buildings

Those servers live in data centres.

📱 Your phone→ 🌐 Internet→ 🏢 Data centre→ 🖥️ Server
  • Huge warehouses with thousands of computers stacked in racks.
  • Massive cooling + backup power, running 24/7.
  • Real places — Mumbai, Hyderabad, and all around the world.
Katixo KhojLab
Idea 3 / 4 — You rent, you don't own

You rent it — you don't buy it.

  • Rent computing & storage instead of buying your own machines.
  • Reach your stuff from any device, anywhere.
  • It scales instantly when you need more.
  • Someone else handles the hardware & maintenance.
🌍 access anywhere
📈 scales instantly
🔧 no maintenance

Like an OYO room vs buying a flat — use it, pay for it, walk away.

Katixo KhojLab
Idea 4 / 4 — You already live in the cloud

You use it every single day.

📷 Google Photos 💬 WhatsApp backup 🎬 Netflix ✉️ Gmail
  • All of these live as files on servers — not really on your phone.
  • They stream to you on demand over the internet.
  • "Cloud" = renting space online + reaching it from anywhere.
Katixo KhojLab
Why it matters

Now a few things make sense.

  • "Storage full — buy more cloud" = renting more space on those servers.
  • No internet? You can't reach the cloud — your stuff is far away.
  • Backups survive even if your phone breaks — the copy lives in a data centre.
  • Privacy angle: your files sit on someone else's computer.

✗ Myth

✓ Reality

It's physical servers in big buildings, owned by companies, reached over the internet. Very much on the ground.
