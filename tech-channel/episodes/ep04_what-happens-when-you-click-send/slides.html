Idea 1 / 4 — Break it up

Your message gets chopped up.

"Hi, how are you? 📷" →
📦 1📦 2📦 3 📦 4…
  • Too big to send as one blob.
  • It's split into small chunks called packets.
  • One photo can become thousands of packets.
Katixo KhojLab
Idea 2 / 4 — Address & number every piece

Each packet carries a label.

# one packet
to:   142.250.195.78
from: your phone
no:   3 of 7
data: …a chunk of the photo…
  • Where it's going + where it's from (IP addresses).
  • A sequence number — "3 of 7".
  • And one small piece of the actual data.
Katixo KhojLab
Idea 3 / 4 — Like Mumbai's dabbawalas

Packets travel separately.

📦→ router → router → 🏢
📦→ a different route → 🏢
  • Like dabbawalas: thousands of tiffins, coded labels, different trains — still reach the right desk.
  • Each packet hops router to router, picking its own path.
  • So they can arrive out of order.
Katixo KhojLab
Idea 4 / 4 — Put it back together

At the end, sorted back in order.

📦 3📦 1📦 4📦 2 → sort → ✅ message
  • The numbers snap the pieces back in order.
  • A packet went missing? It's politely re-requested.
  • You see the whole message — instantly.

Why it matters

This makes the internet unbreakable.

  • A path goes down? Packets just reroute around it.
  • Lost a little? Re-send a few packets — that's buffering, not total failure.
  • One cable carries millions of conversations at once — all chopped & mixed.

Common myth — "yeh galat samajhte hain"
✗ Myth

"My message travels as one whole thing, along one wire, start to finish."

✓ Reality

It's split into packets, each routed independently — maybe by different paths — and reassembled at the end. That system is TCP/IP.

Katixo KhojLab
Recap — 2 things to remember
  1. Your data is split into packets — each labelled with addresses + a number.
  2. They travel separately and are reassembled in order at the other end. That's TCP/IP.
