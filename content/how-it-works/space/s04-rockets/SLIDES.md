## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** ISRO Chandrayaan-3 launch photo or dramatic rocket launch with flame trail
- **Text overlay:** "How Do Rockets ESCAPE Earth?" in bold white + orange glow
- **Stat:** "Escape velocity: 11.2 km/sec — 16× faster than a bullet"
- **Bottom bar:** Katixo KhojLab logo + "S04" badge
- **Mood:** Patriotic, awe-inspiring, dramatic

## Slide 2 — Newton's Third Law
- **Visual:** Rocket diagram showing:
  - Fuel burning inside combustion chamber
  - Hot gas shooting DOWN (labeled "Action")
  - Rocket pushed UP (labeled "Reaction")
- **Text overlay:** "Every Action → Equal & Opposite Reaction"
- **Note:** "Works even BETTER in vacuum — no air resistance"
- **Style:** Physics diagram with force arrows, clean and bold

## Slide 3 — Escape Velocity
- **Visual:** Speed comparison infographic (horizontal bar chart):
  - Fastest car: 0.13 km/s
  - AK-47 bullet: 0.7 km/s
  - Escape velocity: 11.2 km/s (bar extends way off screen)
- **Text overlay:** "11.2 km/s — The Speed to Break Free"
- **Style:** Dramatic scale comparison, rocket icon at the end

## Slide 4 — Escape Velocity Formula
- **Visual:** Formula displayed large and clean: v = √(2GM/R)
- **Labels:** G = gravitational constant, M = planet mass, R = distance from centre
- **Key insight box:** "Mass of rocket NOT in formula — but heavier = more fuel needed"
- **Style:** Chalkboard or clean math aesthetic, each variable color-coded

## Slide 5 — Tyranny of the Rocket Equation
- **Visual:** Pie chart showing rocket mass breakdown:
  - Fuel: ~85-90% (huge red slice)
  - Structure: ~8% 
  - Payload: ~2-5% (tiny slice)
- **ISRO callout:** "GSLV Mk III: 640 tonnes total → 10 tonnes payload (1.5%)"
- **Text overlay:** "The Tyranny of the Rocket Equation"
- **Style:** Dramatic pie chart, tiny payload slice highlighted

## Slide 6 — Rocket Stages
- **Visual:** Rocket stage separation diagram (PSLV style):
  - Stage 1 (solid) → separates → Stage 2 (liquid) → separates → Stage 3 → Stage 4 → Payload
- **Mountain climbing metaphor:** Small figure dropping heavy bags at each camp
- **Text overlay:** "Drop the Weight. Gain Efficiency."
- **Style:** Technical cutaway diagram with separation arrows

## Slide 7 — Launch Physics
- **Visual:** Trajectory diagram from launchpad to orbit showing:
  - Thick atmosphere at bottom → thins out
  - Gravity arrow weakening with altitude
  - G-force meter showing increase to ~3G
- **Text overlay:** "Lighter rocket + Less air + Less gravity = Faster acceleration"
- **Style:** Altitude cross-section, gradient atmosphere layers

## Slide 8 — Orbit = Falling Sideways
- **Visual:** Famous Newton's cannonball thought experiment:
  - Cannon on mountain → slow ball falls to ground → faster ball goes further → fast enough ball keeps falling around Earth
- **ISS callout:** "The ISS is falling toward Earth right now — it just keeps missing"
- **Text overlay:** "Orbit = Falling Around the Earth"
- **Style:** Classic physics diagram, Earth curvature with projectile paths

## Slide 9 — Orbital Mechanics Paradox
- **Visual:** Two orbital rings around Earth:
  - Lower orbit (ISS): 7.8 km/s — faster
  - Higher orbit (Moon): 1.0 km/s — slower
- **Mind-blown callout:** "To go higher, you slow down?!"
- **Text overlay:** "Kepler's Third Law — Higher = Slower"
- **Style:** Orbital diagram, Earth at centre, speed labels on each orbit

## Slide 10 — Rocket Fuel Types
- **Visual:** Side-by-side comparison:
  - Solid fuel: Giant firework block, "Can't stop once lit", "Massive thrust", "Simple/cheap"
  - Liquid fuel: Separate tanks (H₂ + O₂), "Throttle control", "Restartable", "Complex"
- **ISRO note:** "Vikas engine uses UDMH + N₂O₄"
- **Style:** Engineering comparison layout, cutaway fuel diagrams

## Slide 11 — Reusable Rockets
