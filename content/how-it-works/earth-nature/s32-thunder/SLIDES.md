# S32 — Why Does Thunder Come After Lightning? — Slide Design Brief

**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo KhojLab | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---

## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** Dramatic night sky with a bright lightning bolt; a person counting fingers "1...2...3"
- **Text overlay:** "Thunder AFTER Lightning — Why?" in bold white + neon yellow glow
- **Bottom bar:** "Katixo KhojLab" logo + "S32" badge
- **Style:** Cinematic storm, rain streaks, electric mood

## Slide 2 — How Lightning Forms (Charge)
- **Visual:** Cross-section of a storm cloud, ice crystals and water drops colliding; positive charges top, negative bottom
- **Highlight:** "+ at top, − at bottom", small comb-and-hair static analogy inset
- **Text overlay:** "Giant static electricity"
- **Style:** Blue-grey cloud, glowing charge symbols

## Slide 3 — The Bolt Strikes
- **Visual:** Massive lightning bolt jumping cloud-to-ground
- **Highlight:** "~300 million volts", "~30,000 °C = 5x Sun's surface"
- **Text overlay:** "Hotter than the Sun"
- **Style:** Intense white-purple bolt on black sky

## Slide 4 — Air Explodes = Thunder
- **Visual:** Bolt heating surrounding air which violently expands as a shockwave ring, sound waves rippling to an ear
- **Highlight:** Label "shockwave", "thunder = sound of the bolt"
- **Text overlay:** "One event, two parts: light + sound"
- **Style:** Expanding neon shock rings

## Slide 5 — Speed of Light vs Sound
- **Visual:** Race graphic — light beam zooming far ahead, sound wave lagging behind
- **Highlight:** "Light = 300,000 km/s", "Sound = 343 m/s (~1 km in 3 s)"
- **Text overlay:** "Light wins the race"
- **Style:** Split race-track layout, neon speed lines

## Slide 6 — Measure the Distance
- **Visual:** Kid counting seconds with a stopwatch, formula box
- **Highlight:** "Seconds ÷ 3 = distance in km", example "6 s = ~2 km"
- **Text overlay:** "Count and find the storm's distance"
- **Style:** Friendly diagram, neon green formula

## Slide 7 — Why Thunder Rumbles
- **Visual:** Long jagged bolt several km long; sound arriving from near and far parts at different times; echoes off hills/buildings
- **Highlight:** "near sound first, far sound later", "echo = rumble"
- **Text overlay:** "Why it rumbles so long"
- **Style:** Wide landscape, layered sound arcs

## Slide 8 — Safety: Danger Zone
- **Visual:** Warning scene — person leaving open field/tree, going into solid building; under-3-seconds gap alert
- **Highlight:** "Gap < 3 s = storm within ~1 km = DANGER"
- **Text overlay:** "Science can save your life"
- **Style:** Red alert accents, caution icons

## Slide 9 — Myth Buster
- **Visual:** "MYTH" stamp over thunder; a globe with many lightning flashes
- **Highlight:** "Thunder = just sound (harmless)", "Earth: 40–50 strikes every second"
- **Text overlay:** "Lightning is the danger, not thunder"
- **Style:** Bold myth-vs-fact split

## Slide 10 — Full Story Recap Chain
- **Visual:** Flow diagram — Charge → Bolt → 30,000°C → Air explodes → Shockwave → Thunder; light fast / sound slow
- **Text overlay:** "The whole story in one line"
- **Style:** Connected neon flowchart, dark background

## Slide 11 — Heat Lightning
- **Visual:** Far-off lightning flash with NO sound reaching a distant viewer; sound fading out over distance
- **Highlight:** "Sound fades beyond ~16 km", "Heat lightning = flash, no thunder"
- **Text overlay:** "Flash but no boom?"
- **Style:** Distant horizon, soft glow

## Slide 12 — Quiz
- **Visual:** Big neon "Quick Brain Check!" banner + pause icon
- **Text overlay:** Three questions (why flash first? / 9 seconds = how far? / what makes thunder?)
- **Style:** Game-show style, bright yellow, pause prompt corner

## Slide 13 — Answers
- **Visual:** Three checkmark cards revealing answers
- **Text overlay:** "1) Light way faster than sound  2) ~3 km  3) Air suddenly heats & expands"
- **Style:** Green tick reveals on dark cards

## Slide 14 — Recap + Next Episode

- **Text overlay:** "Next: How do noise-cancelling headphones work? + Subscribe!"
