# S44 — Why Do We Have Seasons? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_013.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A four-panel tree showing summer, autumn, winter, spring; a glowing Sun above changing intensity.
- **Text overlay:** "Why Do We Have SEASONS?"
- **Style:** Big neon-cyan title, dark space backdrop, vivid seasonal colour shift.

## Slide 2 — Myth Buster: Not Distance
- **Visual:** Earth's slightly elliptical orbit with "closest in January" marked while India shows winter; big crossed-out distance arrow.
- **Text overlay:** "MYTH: closer = summer (WRONG!)"
- **Style:** Red X over distance idea, neon orbit path, surprise-fact tag.

## Slide 3 — The 23.5° Tilt
- **Visual:** Earth as a tilted spinning top at 23.5°, axis line drawn; comparison with an upright Earth.
- **Text overlay:** "Real reason = 23.5° axial TILT"
- **Style:** Glowing tilted axis, spinning-top metaphor, neon angle label.

## Slide 4 — Tilt Through the Orbit
- **Visual:** Earth at four orbit positions around the Sun, tilt always same direction; N hemisphere toward/away highlighted.
- **Text overlay:** "Tilt toward Sun = summer | away = winter"
- **Style:** Orbit diagram, neon Sun centre, hemisphere glow cues.

## Slide 5 — Sunlight Angle
- **Visual:** Torch beam hitting a surface straight (concentrated bright spot) vs slanted (spread, weak); Sun-rays version.
- **Text overlay:** "Direct rays = concentrated heat"
- **Style:** Torch-beam demo, neon hot-spot vs spread-out glow, dark theme.

## Slide 6 — Day Length
- **Visual:** Summer long-day vs winter short-day clock arcs over a horizon; Sun path high vs low.
- **Text overlay:** "Longer days = more heating time"
- **Style:** Neon sun-arc paths, day/night bar comparison, warm vs cool tint.

## Slide 7 — Solstices & Equinoxes
- **Visual:** Calendar wheel marking Jun 21 (summer solstice), Dec 21 (winter solstice), Mar & Sep (equinoxes).
- **Text overlay:** "Solstices & Equinoxes mapped"
- **Style:** Circular neon timeline, longest/shortest-day icons, dark backdrop.

## Slide 8 — Equator vs Poles
- **Visual:** Globe — equator with steady near-vertical rays (little change), poles with midnight sun and polar night.
- **Text overlay:** "Equator = steady | Poles = extreme"
- **Style:** Split globe zones, neon ray angles, dramatic polar lighting.

## Slide 9 — Seasonal Lag
- **Visual:** A stove turned off with water still heating; graph showing hottest day after Jun 21.
- **Text overlay:** "Hottest day comes LATER (seasonal lag)"
- **Style:** Heat-retention metaphor, neon delay graph, dark theme.

## Slide 10 — Big Takeaway
- **Visual:** Side-by-side: tilted Earth (seasons) vs upright Earth (no seasons, same weather everywhere).
- **Text overlay:** "No tilt = no seasons!"
- **Style:** Contrast panels, neon emphasis on tilt, festive seasonal icons.

## Slide 11 — QUIZ
- **Visual:** Glowing brain icon with three numbered season-themed question cards. Pause symbol.
- **Text overlay:** "Quick Brain Check! | 1·2·3 → PAUSE"
- **Style:** Neon-yellow energetic accents, countdown vibe, dark backdrop.

## Slide 12 — ANSWERS
- **Visual:** Three answer cards with green ticks, mini-icons echoing Slides 3, 4, 8.
- **Text overlay:** "Answers: Tilt · India=Summer→Aus=Winter · Equator Steady"
- **Style:** Green checkmarks, clean reveal layout, dark theme.

## Slide 13 — RECAP / Next Episode
- **Visual:** Recap montage of tilted Earth + Sun-ray icons; teaser of an airplane in clouds for next episode; glowing Subscribe button.
- **Text overlay:** "Next: How Do Planes Stay in the Air? | SUBSCRIBE"
- **Style:** Bold Katixo Shiksha logo, neon Subscribe CTA, upbeat dark finish.
