# S52 — How Do Screens Make Every Color? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo KhojLab | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A glowing smartphone/TV screen at center bursting into a vibrant rainbow spray of colors. Dark space-like background with subtle particle glow. A magnified pixel grid faintly visible in one corner hinting at the secret.
- **Text overlay:** "How do screens make EVERY color?"
- **Style:** Huge bold neon title font (cyan-to-magenta gradient). Katixo KhojLab logo top-left. High contrast, energetic, cinematic glow.

---
## Slide 2 — What Is a Pixel
- **Visual:** A photo zooming progressively into a screen until it dissolves into a grid of tiny square dots. A callout box: "1920 × 1080 ≈ 2 million pixels." Mosaic-art comparison thumbnail on the side.
- **Text overlay:** "PIXEL = Picture Element"
- **Style:** Clean grid graphic, neon-green gridlines on dark bg. Number "~2,000,000" highlighted in bold yellow.

---
## Slide 3 — Sub-Pixels (RGB)
- **Visual:** One pixel hugely magnified, splitting into three vertical stripes — Red, Green, Blue. A waterdrop-on-screen macro photo showing real sub-pixels beside it.
- **Text overlay:** "Every pixel = R + G + B sub-pixels"
- **Style:** Three bold stripes in pure red, green, blue with glow. Label "RGB" in large letters at bottom.

---
## Slide 4 — Additive Color Mixing
- **Visual:** Classic three overlapping light circles (red, green, blue) on black. Overlaps clearly labeled: R+G = Yellow, G+B = Cyan, B+R = Magenta, center = White.
- **Text overlay:** "Adding LIGHT makes new colors"
- **Style:** Glowing overlapping spotlights on pure black background. Overlap labels in crisp white text.

---
## Slide 5 — RGB vs RYB (Light vs Paint)
- **Visual:** Split-screen. Left: paint palette / mixing buckets labeled "Subtractive (RYB)". Right: glowing light beams labeled "Additive (RGB)". Big arrow contrast between them.
- **Text overlay:** "Paint absorbs light — Screens emit it"
- **Style:** Left side warm/matte paint texture, right side glowing dark neon. Clear visual divider down the middle.

---
## Slide 6 — Brightness 0–255
- **Visual:** Three vertical slider knobs labeled R, G, B, each with a scale from 0 to 255. A live color swatch updating based on slider positions.
- **Text overlay:** "Each sub-pixel: 0 → 255 (256 levels)"
- **Style:** Sleek dark UI sliders with neon fill. Volume-knob analogy icon. Bold "256" emphasized.

---
## Slide 7 — The Math: 16.7 Million Colors
- **Visual:** Big equation graphic: 256 × 256 × 256 = 16,777,216. A swirling field of millions of colored dots behind it.
- **Text overlay:** "256³ ≈ 16.7 MILLION colors"
- **Style:** Giant glowing equation in monospace font. Result "16.7M" in massive gradient text. Dark cosmic backdrop.

---
## Slide 8 — White vs Black
- **Visual:** Two pixels side by side. Left: all three sub-pixels at 255, glowing pure white. Right: all three at 0, pure black. Labels "ON ON ON = WHITE" and "OFF OFF OFF = BLACK."
- **Text overlay:** "All ON = White • All OFF = Black"
- **Style:** Strong contrast composition. White pixel radiates glow; black pixel sits in deep shadow.

---
## Slide 9 — Eyes Blend the Dots (PPI)
- **Visual:** Same image shown at low PPI (visible dots) vs high PPI (smooth). A magnifier reveals dots; from a distance they blend. Callout: "400+ PPI on phones."
- **Text overlay:** "Your eyes BLEND tiny sub-pixels"
- **Style:** Side-by-side comparison with a sliding magnifier graphic. "PPI = Pixels Per Inch" label in neon.

---
## Slide 10 — LCD vs OLED
- **Visual:** Split diagram. Left: LCD with a constant white backlight panel behind colored filters. Right: OLED with each pixel self-emitting, some fully off for true black.
- **Text overlay:** "LCD = backlight • OLED = self-lit pixels"
- **Style:** Technical cutaway illustration, glowing arrows for light paths. OLED side shows deep black region.

---
## Slide 11 — Putting It All Together
- **Visual:** Infographic flow: 2M pixels → R/G/B sub-pixels → 0–255 brightness → 16.7M colors → blended by eye into a real photo (sunset/face). Chain of icons connected by glowing arrows.
- **Text overlay:** "3 colors → millions of shades"
- **Style:** Clean horizontal flow infographic, neon connectors, final photo glowing at the end.

---
## Slide 12 — Quiz
- **Visual:** Large "QUICK BRAIN CHECK!" banner with a pause icon. Three numbered question cards stacked.
- **Text overlay:** "1. The 3 primary screen colors?  2. Brightness range of a sub-pixel?  3. Red + Green light = ?"
- **Style:** Game-show vibe, bold neon question cards on dark bg. Big pulsing pause-button icon.

---
## Slide 13 — Answers
- **Visual:** Same three cards now flipped to reveal answers with green check marks.
- **Text overlay:** "1. Red, Green, Blue (RGB)  2. 0 to 255 (256 levels)  3. Yellow"
- **Style:** Green check-mark accents, satisfying reveal layout matching slide 12 design.

---
## Slide 14 — Recap & Wrap-Up
- **Visual:** Quick recap mosaic of key icons (pixel grid, RGB stripes, 0–255 sliders, 16.7M, white/black, eye, LCD/OLED). Katixo KhojLab logo prominent. Subscribe button and bell icon glowing.
- **Text overlay:** "3 colors. Millions of shades. SUBSCRIBE!"
- **Style:** Celebratory recap collage, bold call-to-action. Glowing red Subscribe button and bell. Warm energetic finish.
