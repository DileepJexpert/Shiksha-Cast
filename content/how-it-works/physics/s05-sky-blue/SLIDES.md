## Slide 2 — Sunlight Is a Mix
- **Visual:** Prism splitting white sunlight into rainbow spectrum (classic Newton diagram)
- **Wavelength scale:** Red (700nm) → Orange → Yellow → Green → Blue (475nm) → Violet (380nm)
- **Text overlay:** "White Light = ALL Colours Mixed"
- **Key note:** "Shorter wavelength = Higher energy"
- **Style:** Clean optics diagram, rainbow on dark background

## Slide 3 — Earth's Atmosphere
- **Visual:** Earth cross-section showing atmosphere layers with composition:
  - 78% Nitrogen (N₂) — dominant
  - 21% Oxygen (O₂)
  - 1% other (Ar, CO₂, H₂O)
- **Zoom callout:** Single N₂ molecule (~0.3nm) next to light wave (~475nm) — huge size difference
- **Text overlay:** "Molecules Much Smaller Than Light Waves"
- **Style:** Atmospheric layers diagram, labeled gas percentages

## Slide 4 — Rayleigh Scattering
- **Visual:** Diagram showing:
  - Sunlight hitting a small molecule
  - Blue light scattered in all directions (many scattered arrows)
  - Red light mostly passing straight through (one straight arrow)
- **Formula callout:** "Intensity ∝ 1/λ⁴" (inverse fourth power)
- **Text overlay:** "Blue scattered 16× MORE than red"
- **Style:** Physics diagram with scattered/transmitted ray paths

## Slide 5 — Why the Sky Is Blue (Full Picture)
- **Visual:** Sky dome diagram:
  - Sun on one side, person looking up on ground
  - Blue arrows scattered all across the sky dome
  - Red/yellow arrows passing straight through
  - Person's eyes receiving scattered blue from all directions
- **Text overlay:** "Blue Light Bounces Everywhere. You See It From Every Direction."
- **Style:** Panoramic sky diagram, blue scatter pattern

## Slide 6 — Why Not Violet?
- **Visual:** Two-part explanation:
  - Graph 1: Solar emission spectrum showing peak in blue-green (more blue photons than violet)
  - Graph 2: Human eye sensitivity curve (cone response) — strong blue response, weak violet
- **Text overlay:** "Physics + Biology = Blue, Not Violet"
- **Style:** Dual graph layout, labeled curves, violet vs blue highlighted

## Slide 7 — Sunset Explained
- **Visual:** Comparison diagram:
  - Noon: short atmospheric path → blue scattered, plenty of all colours reach you
  - Sunset: very long atmospheric path → ALL blue scattered away → only red/orange/yellow survive
- **Text overlay:** "Sunset = Light That Survived the Longest Journey"
- **Style:** Side-by-side Earth cross-sections, warm sunset tones on one side

## Slide 8 — Sky Colours on Other Planets
- **Visual:** 3-panel comparison:
  - Earth: Blue sky, red sunset
  - Mars: Pink/butterscotch sky, BLUE sunset (NASA rover photo reference)
  - Moon: BLACK sky (no atmosphere) even during day
- **Text overlay:** "Mars Has Blue Sunsets. Mind = Blown."
- **Style:** Planetary comparison, each panel with planet label and sky colour

## Slide 9 — Horizon vs Zenith Gradient
- **Visual:** Photo or illustration of sky showing clear gradient:
  - Overhead (zenith): deep blue
  - Horizon: pale/whitish
- **Diagram:** Light path length comparison — short path overhead, long path at horizon
- **Text overlay:** "More Atmosphere = More Scattering = Paler Sky"
- **Style:** Gradient sky photo with explanatory overlay

## Slide 10 — Mie Scattering (Clouds)
- **Visual:** Comparison diagram:
  - Rayleigh: small particle (molecule) → scatters blue more → blue sky
  - Mie: large particle (water droplet) → scatters ALL colours equally → white clouds
- **Rain cloud note:** "Thick clouds scatter so much light → grey/dark underneath"
- **Text overlay:** "Rayleigh = Blue Sky. Mie = White Clouds."
- **Style:** Side-by-side particle-and-scattering diagrams

## Slide 11 — The Physics Behind λ⁴
- **Visual:** Simplified electromagnetic explanation:
  - Light wave (oscillating electric field) hits molecule
  - Electrons forced to oscillate at same frequency
  - Oscillating charge re-radiates light
  - Power radiated ∝ frequency⁴ ∝ 1/wavelength⁴
- **Historical note:** "Rayleigh (1871) + Einstein (1910)"
- **Style:** Physics diagram with wave + particle interaction

## Slide 12 — Sky Colour = Air Quality
- **Visual:** Side-by-side photos:
  - Deep vivid blue sky = Clean air (mostly Rayleigh scattering)
  - Hazy whitish sky = Polluted air (Mie scattering from particles)
- **Practical tip:** "After rain → cleanest, bluest sky"
- **Text overlay:** "Blue Sky = Clean Air"
- **Style:** Real photo comparison, air quality indicator
