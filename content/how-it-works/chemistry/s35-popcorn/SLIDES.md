# S35 — Why Does Popcorn Pop? — Slide Design Brief

**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo KhojLab | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---

## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** Hard yellow kernels in a hot pan mid-pop, white fluffy popcorn bursting upward with motion trails
- **Text overlay:** "Why Does POPCORN Pop?" in bold white + neon orange glow
- **Bottom bar:** "Katixo KhojLab" logo + "S35" badge
- **Style:** Warm, appetizing, dynamic burst

## Slide 2 — A Special Corn
- **Visual:** Comparison — sweet corn / bhutta (won't pop) vs popcorn kernels (will pop); tough shell highlighted
- **Highlight:** "Zea mays everta", "extra-hard airtight shell = pericarp"
- **Text overlay:** "Not every corn pops"
- **Style:** Side-by-side, neon labels

## Slide 3 — Inside a Kernel
- **Visual:** Cross-section of one kernel — hard pericarp shell, soft starch inside, a trapped water droplet at center
- **Highlight:** "pericarp", "starch", "~13–14% water"
- **Text overlay:** "Water is the secret fuel"
- **Style:** Clean cutaway, glowing water drop

## Slide 4 — Heat: Steam Builds
- **Visual:** Kernel on heat; water turning to steam, trapped inside by the shell; pressure-cooker analogy inset
- **Highlight:** "past 100 °C", "steam trapped, no escape", "pressure rising"
- **Text overlay:** "A tiny pressure cooker"
- **Style:** Red heat glow, rising pressure arrows

## Slide 5 — Extreme Pressure
- **Visual:** Kernel glowing hot; gauge showing high pressure; starch melting into hot gel
- **Highlight:** "~180 °C inside", "~9x outside pressure", "starch melts to gel"
- **Text overlay:** "180°C and 9x the pressure!"
- **Style:** Intense gauge graphic, neon red/orange

## Slide 6 — POP!
- **Visual:** Shell bursting, steam exploding out, starch puffing and instantly cooling/setting white
- **Highlight:** "shell can't hold", "starch puffs & sets in cool air"
- **Text overlay:** "Boom — fluffy popcorn!"
- **Style:** Big burst, motion lines, bright flash

## Slide 7 — It Turns Inside-Out
- **Visual:** Diagram showing inner starch now outside, the old shell folded into the center (the bit stuck in teeth)
- **Highlight:** "expands 40–50x its size", "shell ends up inside"
- **Text overlay:** "Popcorn flips inside-out"
- **Style:** Before/after flip diagram

## Slide 8 — Why Some Don't Pop
- **Visual:** A few unpopped kernels labelled "old maids"; one with low water, one with a cracked shell leaking steam
- **Highlight:** "old maids = low water OR cracked shell"
- **Text overlay:** "The duds: too dry or leaky"
- **Style:** Sad unpopped kernels, leak arrows

## Slide 9 — Storing Popcorn Right
- **Visual:** Open jar losing moisture vs airtight jar keeping kernels fresh; a "rehydrate" jugaad tip
- **Highlight:** "keep airtight", "lost water = no pop", "rehydrate trick"
- **Text overlay:** "Kitchen science hack"
- **Style:** Practical infographic, teal accents

## Slide 10 — Ancient Snack
- **Visual:** Old archaeological dig with ancient popcorn kernels; timeline showing thousands of years
- **Highlight:** "eaten by native Americans", "thousands of years old"
- **Text overlay:** "One of the oldest snacks ever"
- **Style:** Earthy, historical, neon timeline

## Slide 11 — Full Story Recap Chain
- **Visual:** Flow diagram — Special corn → trapped water → steam → pressure → starch melts → POP → cools & sets
- **Text overlay:** "The whole pop in one line"
- **Style:** Connected neon flowchart, dark background

## Slide 12 — Quiz
- **Visual:** Big neon "Quick Brain Check!" banner + pause icon

- **Style:** Game-show style, bright orange, pause prompt corner

## Slide 13 — Answers
