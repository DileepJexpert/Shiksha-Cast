# S34 — Why Do We Feel Deja Vu? — Slide Design Brief

**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---

## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A person freezing mid-moment with a glowing "rewind" echo of themselves; dreamy double-exposure effect
- **Text overlay:** "Why Do We Feel DEJA VU?" in bold white + neon magenta glow
- **Bottom bar:** "Katixo Shiksha" logo + "S34" badge
- **Style:** Surreal, mysterious, double-image

## Slide 2 — It's Super Common
- **Visual:** Crowd of people, ~two-thirds glowing to show how many feel it; age band highlighted
- **Highlight:** "60–70% of people", "most common ages 15–25", "lasts a few seconds"
- **Text overlay:** "You're not alone — it's normal"
- **Style:** Infographic crowd, neon stats

## Slide 3 — Familiarity vs Memory
- **Visual:** Brain with hippocampus highlighted; two icons — a "feels familiar" heart and a "where/when" memory tag, normally linked
- **Highlight:** "hippocampus", "familiarity vs actual memory"
- **Text overlay:** "Familiar... but no memory attached"
- **Style:** Clean brain diagram, glowing region

## Slide 4 — Dual Processing Theory
- **Visual:** Two parallel signal paths into the brain; one path slightly delayed, registering the moment twice
- **Highlight:** "tiny time-lag", "moment registered twice (milliseconds apart)"
- **Text overlay:** "Your brain double-saves the moment"
- **Style:** Two neon channels, one lagging

## Slide 5 — Gestalt Familiarity
- **Visual:** A new room whose layout (window, bed, curtains) overlaps faintly with a forgotten old room
- **Highlight:** "same layout, forgotten source"
- **Text overlay:** "New place, old shape"
- **Style:** Ghosted overlay of two rooms

## Slide 6 — Hidden Memories
- **Visual:** Brain shelf with dusty forgotten items — a movie frame, a photo, a dream cloud — one lighting up faintly
- **Highlight:** "forgotten dream/movie/book", "cryptomnesia (hidden memory)"
- **Text overlay:** "A buried memory sneaks back"
- **Style:** Dim archive shelves, single glow

## Slide 7 — Inside the Brain (Temporal Lobe)
- **Visual:** Brain with temporal lobe highlighted; small electrical spark near it
- **Highlight:** "temporal lobe epilepsy clue", "tiny electrical spark triggers familiarity"
- **Text overlay:** "A mini spark of familiarity"
- **Style:** Anatomical, neon spark accent

## Slide 8 — Myth Buster (Not the Future)
- **Visual:** "MYTH" stamp over a crystal ball / "I saw it in a dream"; reveal: it's a memory illusion
- **Highlight:** "Not prediction", bonus "jamais vu = familiar feels strange"
- **Text overlay:** "It's not predicting the future"
- **Style:** Bold myth-vs-fact split

## Slide 9 — When It Happens More
- **Visual:** Tired/stressed person, sleepless icon; plus a traveler with many movie/book icons
- **Highlight:** "tired, stressed, low sleep", "more travel/movies = more deja vu"
- **Text overlay:** "Tired brains slip more"
- **Style:** Two-panel cause infographic

## Slide 10 — It's a GOOD Sign
- **Visual:** VR-headset lab experiment recreating deja vu; brain doing a "fact-check" tick on a memory
- **Highlight:** "VR experiments", "brain checking: did this really happen?"
- **Text overlay:** "Your memory is fact-checking itself"
- **Style:** Bright, positive teal accents

## Slide 11 — It Fades With Age
- **Visual:** Graph of deja vu frequency high in teens, dropping after 40–50; young vibrant brain icon
- **Highlight:** "peaks in teens/young adults", "sharp young brain catches mismatches"
- **Text overlay:** "Sign of a sharp young brain"
- **Style:** Neon line graph, dark grid

## Slide 12 — Quiz
- **Visual:** Big neon "Quick Brain Check!" banner + pause icon
- **Text overlay:** Three questions (which feeling appears w/o memory? / which brain part? / opposite of deja vu?)
- **Style:** Game-show style, bright magenta, pause prompt corner

## Slide 13 — Answers
- **Visual:** Three checkmark cards revealing answers
- **Text overlay:** "1) Familiarity  2) Temporal lobe  3) Jamais vu"
- **Style:** Green tick reveals on dark cards

## Slide 14 — Recap + Next Episode
- **Visual:** Montage — double-exposure moment, temporal lobe spark, VR test; tease image of popping popcorn
- **Text overlay:** "Next: Why does popcorn pop? + Subscribe!"
- **Bottom bar:** "Katixo Shiksha" logo + Subscribe button graphic
- **Style:** Energetic outro, neon call-to-action
