# S12 — Why Do We Cry? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** Split-screen close-up of a single human eye with a glistening teardrop on the lash. Left half shows a cut onion releasing wispy vapor; right half shows a blurred sad movie scene reflected in the eye. A neon glow line splits the two.
- **Text overlay:** "Why Do We Cry?" (huge) with small kicker "Same tears, totally different reasons"
- **Style:** Cinematic dark background, cyan and magenta neon split, bold white title, Katixo Shiksha logo bottom-right.

---
## Slide 2 — You Are Always Crying
- **Visual:** Cross-section illustration of an eye with lacrimal gland highlighted above it, glowing. Faint animation-style arrows showing a thin tear film spreading across the eye as the eyelid blinks. A counter graphic: "15,000–20,000 blinks/day".
- **Text overlay:** "You're crying right now — tiny tears, every blink"
- **Style:** Dark teal background, neon cyan glands, clean diagram labels.

---
## Slide 3 — What Are Tears Made Of?
- **Visual:** A magnified teardrop shown as a transparent sphere broken into labeled segments: Water (largest), Salt, Oils, Antibodies (lysozyme). Small bacteria icons being zapped/destroyed by a glowing lysozyme molecule.
- **Text overlay:** "Tears = Water + Salt + Oils + Bacteria-killing Lysozyme"
- **Style:** Dark background, neon green for lysozyme, infographic pie-style callouts.

---
## Slide 4 — The 3 Layers of a Tear
- **Visual:** Zoomed sandwich-style cross-section of the tear film on the eye surface: top Oily layer, middle Watery layer (thickest), bottom Mucus layer touching the eye. Labeled with thin neon connector lines.
- **Text overlay:** "One tear, three smart layers"
- **Style:** Dark gradient, layered translucent bands in cyan/blue/purple, crisp labels.

---
## Slide 5 — Three Types of Tears
- **Visual:** Three glowing panels side by side: (1) Basal — calm eye with steady drip icon; (2) Reflex — onion + dust burst; (3) Emotional — a face with a heart/brain glow. Each panel a different neon color.
- **Text overlay:** "Basal · Reflex · Emotional — 3 kinds of tears"
- **Style:** Dark triptych layout, three distinct neon accents, bold category labels.

---
## Slide 6 — Basal Tears (The Everyday Heroes)
- **Visual:** Eye with a windshield-wiper analogy overlaid — the eyelid drawn like a car wiper sweeping across a windshield-shaped eye, clearing dust specks. Small oxygen molecule icons reaching the eye surface.
- **Text overlay:** "Basal tears: clean, moisten, protect — 24/7"
- **Style:** Dark blue background, neon cyan wiper motion lines, playful but clean.

---
## Slide 7 — Reflex Tears (Onion Attack!)
- **Visual:** Dynamic shot of a cut onion releasing a glowing gas cloud rising toward a wide, watering eye. Arrow from brain to lacrimal gland labeled "Make more tears — fast!". Dust and smoke icons in corner as other triggers.
- **Text overlay:** "Reflex tears flush out irritants — automatically"
- **Style:** Dark background, neon yellow-green gas cloud, energetic action vibe.

---
## Slide 8 — Emotional Tears (Only Humans)
- **Visual:** Silhouette of a human head with the limbic system highlighted deep in the brain, glowing. A signal line travels from limbic system → nervous system → lacrimal gland → teardrop. A crowd of animal silhouettes faded out with an "only humans" badge.
- **Text overlay:** "Emotions → limbic system → tears (humans only)"
- **Style:** Dark moody background, warm neon orange brain glow, signal pathway in pink.

---
## Slide 9 — The Surprising Chemistry
- **Visual:** Two test tubes labeled "Reflex Tears" and "Emotional Tears" side by side. The emotional one has extra glowing molecule icons labeled ACTH (stress hormone) and Leucine Enkephalin (natural painkiller) floating out and away.
- **Text overlay:** "Emotional tears carry stress hormones + a natural painkiller"
- **Style:** Dark lab background, neon purple/pink molecules, science-poster look.

---
## Slide 10 — Why Crying Makes Your Nose Run
- **Visual:** Front-facing face diagram with a cutaway showing the path: lacrimal gland → eye surface → puncta (tiny holes at inner eye corner) → tear ducts → nose. Glowing dotted "plumbing" line tracing the route, droplets shown reaching the nose.
- **Text overlay:** "Tears drain through puncta into your nose"
- **Style:** Dark background, neon cyan plumbing path, clear anatomical labels.

---
## Slide 11 — The Full Picture (Recap of Mechanism)
- **Visual:** A single clean system map combining everything: lacrimal gland at top, three labeled tear types branching out, contents listed (water, salt, oils, lysozyme), and drainage arrow to nose. Connected like a circuit diagram.
- **Text overlay:** "One perfectly designed tear system"
- **Style:** Dark background, multi-color neon flowchart, balanced composition.

---
## Slide 12 — Quick Brain Check (Quiz)
- **Visual:** Big bold "PAUSE" button graphic with a glowing pause icon. Three numbered question cards stacked. Brain icon thinking with question marks.
- **Text overlay:** "Quick Brain Check! ⏸ Pause now — 3 questions"
- **Style:** Dark background, bright neon yellow quiz cards, high-energy game-show feel.

---
## Slide 13 — Answers
- **Visual:** Three answer cards revealing with green check marks: (1) Lacrimal glands, (2) Lysozyme, (3) Leucine Enkephalin. A "tear expert" badge graphic in the corner.
- **Text overlay:** "Answers: Lacrimal glands · Lysozyme · Leucine Enkephalin"
- **Style:** Dark background, neon green checkmarks, celebratory but clean.

---
## Slide 14 — Recap + Next Episode Tease
