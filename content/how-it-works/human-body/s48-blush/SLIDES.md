# S48 — Why Do We Blush? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A close-up illustration of a teen's face with glowing neon-red cheeks, surrounded by floating question marks and a faint outline of dilated blood vessels under the skin. Dark navy background with a soft red glow around the cheeks.
- **Text overlay:** "Why Do We BLUSH?"
- **Style:** Massive bold neon-red title, Katixo Shiksha logo top-left, energetic comic-style spotlight on the face. High-contrast, attention-grabbing.

---
## Slide 2 — What Is Blushing?
- **Visual:** Split diagram of a cheek cross-section: skin layer on top, a network of tiny blood vessels widening below, with red blood flowing through. Arrows show vessels expanding.
- **Text overlay:** "Vasodilation = vessels widen + more blood = red, warm cheeks"
- **Style:** Clean labeled anatomy diagram, neon-cyan labels on dark background, glowing red blood-flow accents.

---
## Slide 3 — The Sympathetic Nervous System
- **Visual:** A glowing nervous-system silhouette of a human body, with the brain lit up and signal lines firing toward the face. A small "ALARM" badge near the brain.
- **Text overlay:** "Sympathetic Nervous System: your body's alarm switch"
- **Style:** Neon-green nerve pathways on near-black background, electric pulse animation feel, bold caption bar at bottom.

---
## Slide 4 — Adrenaline & Fight-or-Flight
- **Visual:** A stylized hormone molecule labeled "ADRENALINE" bursting outward, with icons of a racing heart, fast breathing, and widening face vessels around it.
- **Text overlay:** "Adrenaline fires the FIGHT-or-FLIGHT response"
- **Style:** Explosive neon-orange burst graphics, dark backdrop, dynamic energy lines radiating outward.

---
## Slide 5 — Why Mainly Face, Cheeks & Neck?
- **Visual:** A human figure with face, cheeks, and neck highlighted in glowing red, the rest of the body dimmed. Inset zoom shows thin, surface-near capillaries.
- **Text overlay:** "More vessels, thinner walls, closer to the surface"
- **Style:** Spotlight contrast (lit face vs dark body), neon-red highlight zones, magnifier inset graphic.

---
## Slide 6 — Blushing Is Involuntary
- **Visual:** A "control panel" with a greyed-out, locked button labeled "BLUSH" and a red crossed-out hand trying to press it. A small autonomic-nervous-system icon glows beside it.
- **Text overlay:** "You can't control it — it's INVOLUNTARY"
- **Style:** Playful UI/control-panel motif, neon-red lock icon, dark dashboard background.

---
## Slide 7 — Uniquely Human
- **Visual:** A row of animal silhouettes (dog, cat, monkey) with neutral faces, next to a single human face glowing red. A small portrait of Charles Darwin in the corner.
- **Text overlay:** "Only HUMANS blush — the most human expression"
- **Style:** Comparison layout, dimmed animals vs glowing human, vintage-style Darwin badge, neon-pink accent.

---
## Slide 8 — Social Emotions Trigger It
- **Visual:** A face at the center with multiple spotlights and eyes pointing at it, surrounded by emotion icons: embarrassment, praise star, heart for crush.
- **Text overlay:** "Embarrassment, praise, crush = all eyes on you"
- **Style:** Stage-spotlight theme, converging beams on the face, neon-yellow emotion icons on dark stage.

---
## Slide 9 — Blushing Builds Trust
- **Visual:** Two figures shaking hands; one has glowing red cheeks with a floating "honest / trustworthy" badge above. A heart-with-checkmark trust icon.
- **Text overlay:** "Blushing signals: I'm honest, I care"
- **Style:** Warm trust-themed glow, neon-green checkmark accents, friendly rounded character art.

---
## Slide 10 — The Fear-of-Blushing Loop
- **Visual:** A circular loop diagram: "Fear" arrow to "Blush" arrow back to "More Fear", spinning around a worried face in the center.
- **Text overlay:** "Fear of blushing → makes you blush MORE"
- **Style:** Glowing circular feedback-loop graphic, neon-red arrows, slightly anxious comic expression in center.

---
## Slide 11 — The Full Chain Reaction
- **Visual:** A horizontal flowchart: Awkward moment → Nervous system → Adrenaline → Vasodilation → Red cheeks. Each step in its own glowing node connected by arrows.
- **Text overlay:** "Moment → Nerves → Adrenaline → Vessels → RED!"
- **Style:** Step-by-step neon flowchart, numbered glowing nodes, dark gradient background, cyan connector arrows.

---
## Slide 12 — Quiz Time
- **Visual:** A glowing brain icon with three numbered question cards floating around it. A big pause-button symbol in a corner.
- **Text overlay:** "Quick Brain Check! Pause & answer (3 Qs)"
- **Style:** Game-show quiz aesthetic, neon-purple question cards, bold playful numbers 1-2-3, dark background.

---
## Slide 13 — Answers
- **Visual:** The same three question cards now flipped to reveal answers, each with a green checkmark. Confetti-style neon sparkles.
- **Text overlay:** "Answers: 1) Widen / vasodilation  2) Adrenaline  3) No!"
- **Style:** Reveal layout with green check accents, celebratory neon sparkles, clear readable answer text on dark cards.

---
## Slide 14 — Recap & Next Episode
- **Visual:** A recap strip of mini-icons (nervous system, adrenaline, red cheeks) on the left; on the right a teaser panel with a glowing solar panel under a sun, labeled "NEXT". Subscribe button graphic at the bottom.
- **Text overlay:** "Next: How do solar panels make electricity? — Subscribe!"
- **Style:** Two-panel split (recap + teaser), neon-red recap side and neon-blue solar teaser side, bold Katixo Shiksha subscribe call-to-action bar.
