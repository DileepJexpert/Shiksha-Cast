# S31 — Why Do We Forget Things? — Slide Design Brief

**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo KhojLab | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---

## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A glowing human head silhouette with bits of light/memory fragments fading and floating away into darkness
- **Text overlay:** "Why Do We FORGET?" in bold white + neon purple glow
- **Bottom bar:** "Katixo KhojLab" logo + "S31" badge
- **Style:** Mysterious, cinematic, fading particles for "lost memory" feel

## Slide 2 — How Memory Forms
- **Visual:** Network of glowing neurons connecting, sparks jumping across synapses
- **Highlight:** Label "86 billion neurons" and "synapse" with neon arrows
- **Text overlay:** "Memory = an electric dance"
- **Style:** Blue-cyan neural web on dark background

## Slide 3 — Short-term vs Long-term
- **Visual:** Two boxes — small flickering box (short-term, "~7 items") feeding into a large glowing vault (long-term)
- **Highlight:** Arrow labelled "Consolidation (happens during sleep)"
- **Text overlay:** "Sleep saves your memories"
- **Style:** Split layout, neon dividers

## Slide 4 — Decay Theory & Forgetting Curve
- **Visual:** A grassy path fading/overgrowing on left; on right, a downward graph (Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve)
- **Highlight:** Mark "1 hour = half forgotten", year "1885", name "Ebbinghaus"
- **Text overlay:** "Use it or lose it"
- **Style:** Graph in neon green, dark grid background

## Slide 5 — Interference
- **Visual:** A crowded library with similar books colliding / two password bubbles clashing
- **Highlight:** Labels "Proactive" (old blocks new) and "Retroactive" (new blocks old)
- **Text overlay:** "Memories crash into each other"
- **Style:** Warm orange clash sparks on dark shelves

## Slide 6 — Retrieval Failure
- **Visual:** A person with a word stuck as a glowing bubble just above the tongue, locked padlock on a memory box
- **Highlight:** "Tip-of-the-tongue", "Cue = the key", "context-dependent memory"
- **Text overlay:** "It's there... just locked"
- **Style:** Single spotlight, mysterious mood

## Slide 7 — Forgetting Is GOOD
- **Visual:** Brain as a desk being cleaned — crumpled junk papers thrown out, important notes kept and glowing
- **Highlight:** Mention "hyperthymesia" as a small caption
- **Text overlay:** "Forgetting = smart filing"
- **Style:** Bright, positive neon teal accents

## Slide 8 — Sleep & Cleanup
- **Visual:** Sleeping person with brain replaying glowing memory loops; tiny "cleaning" streams washing through brain
- **Highlight:** Labels "Replay", "Deep sleep", "glymphatic cleanup"
- **Text overlay:** "Your brain cleans itself at night"
- **Style:** Calm indigo night palette, soft glow

## Slide 9 — Stress & Emotion
- **Visual:** Split image — calm brain (clear retrieval) vs stressed brain with red cortisol haze blocking the hippocampus
- **Highlight:** Label "amygdala" (emotion) and "hippocampus" (memory boss)
- **Text overlay:** "Stress jams your memory"
- **Style:** Cool blue vs hot red contrast

## Slide 10 — How To Forget Less
- **Visual:** Four icons in a row — calendar (spaced repetition), closed book with question mark (active recall), linked chain (association), sleeping moon
- **Text overlay:** "4 science-backed study hacks"
- **Style:** Clean icon grid, neon yellow highlights

## Slide 11 — Memory Is Not a Recording
- **Visual:** A painting being repainted slightly differently each time / two friends remembering one event differently
- **Highlight:** Caption "false memories are real"
- **Text overlay:** "Your brain re-paints memories"
- **Style:** Artistic brush-stroke overlays, dark canvas

## Slide 12 — Quiz
- **Visual:** Big neon "Quick Brain Check!" banner with a pause icon
- **Text overlay:** Three numbered questions (permanent memory cause? / old-blocks-new effect? / Ebbinghaus curve name?)
- **Style:** Game-show style, bright cyan, pause prompt corner

## Slide 13 — Answers
- **Visual:** Three checkmark cards revealing answers
- **Text overlay:** "1) Sleep  2) Proactive interference  3) Forgetting Curve"
- **Style:** Green tick reveals on dark cards

## Slide 14 — Recap + Next Episode
- **Visual:** Quick montage thumbnails of neurons, sleep, study hacks; tease image of lightning bolt for next episode
- **Text overlay:** "Next: Why does thunder come AFTER lightning? + Subscribe!"
- **Bottom bar:** "Katixo KhojLab" logo + Subscribe button graphic
- **Style:** Energetic outro, neon call-to-action
