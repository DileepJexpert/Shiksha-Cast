# S01 — Why Do You Get Goosebumps? — Slide Design Brief

**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---

## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** Close-up photo of arm with goosebumps, dramatic blue-purple lighting
- **Text overlay:** "Why Do You Get GOOSEBUMPS?" in bold white + neon blue glow
- **Bottom bar:** "Katixo Shiksha" logo + "S01" badge
- **Mood:** Mysterious, cinematic, attention-grabbing

## Slide 2 — Skin Structure Intro
- **Visual:** Cross-section diagram of skin showing layers (epidermis, dermis, fat layer)
- **Highlight:** Label the hair follicle and arrector pili muscle with arrow pointers
- **Text overlay:** "Your Skin = Largest Organ" 
- **Style:** Clean anatomical illustration, dark background, colored labels

## Slide 3 — The Mechanism
- **Visual:** Zoomed diagram: arrector pili muscle contracting → hair standing up → bump forming
- **Show 3 steps:** Relaxed → Signal received → Contracted (side-by-side or animated sequence)
- **Text overlay:** "Arrector Pili Muscle → Goosebump"
- **Style:** Before/after comparison, use arrows to show movement

## Slide 4 — Ancient Ancestors (Warmth)
- **Visual:** Split image — hairy ancient human ancestor on left, modern human on right
- **Illustration:** Show fur standing up with warm air trapped between hairs (wavy heat lines)
- **Text overlay:** "Built-in Heating System"
- **Style:** Warm orange/amber tones for the warmth concept

## Slide 5 — Fight or Flight (Intimidation)
- **Visual:** Angry cat with puffed-up fur on one side, silhouette of ancient human with standing fur on other
- **Text overlay:** "Look Bigger. Scare Predators."
- **Bottom text:** "Same mechanism — arrector pili muscles"
- **Style:** Dramatic red/orange tones, danger/threat vibe

## Slide 6 — Vestigial Response
- **Visual:** Smartphone icon showing an "old unused app" metaphor
- **Diagram:** Timeline arrow: Ancient (useful) → Modern (still fires, no longer useful)
- **Text overlay:** "Vestigial Response — Your Body's Legacy Code"
- **Style:** Tech-meets-biology aesthetic, clean infographic

## Slide 7 — Emotional Goosebumps
- **Visual:** Person wearing headphones with eyes closed, musical notes floating, goosebumps on arm
- **Diagram:** Brain icon → dopamine molecules → nerve pathway → skin
- **Text overlay:** "Music + Dopamine = Goosebumps"
- **Style:** Purple/blue emotional tones, dreamy aesthetic

## Slide 8 — Harvard Brain Study
- **Visual:** Brain scan image showing auditory cortex connected to emotional areas with bright fibre lines
- **Text overlay:** "Denser neural connections = Feel music more intensely"
- **Small text:** "Harvard University study"
- **Style:** Neuroscience aesthetic — dark background, glowing brain pathways

## Slide 9 — Autonomic Nervous System
- **Visual:** Nervous system diagram — brain → spinal cord → branching into sympathetic (highlighted) and parasympathetic
- **Text overlay:** "Sympathetic Nervous System — You Can't Control It"
- **Challenge text:** "Try giving yourself goosebumps right now!"
- **Style:** Anatomical diagram, neon green nerve lines on dark background

## Slide 10 — Animals That Use It
- **Visual:** 3-panel grid: Porcupine with raised quills | Pufferfish inflated | Cat with arched back
- **Text overlay:** "Same Mechanism, Different Weapons"
- **Labels:** Under each animal — "Quills" / "Spines" / "Fur"
- **Style:** Wildlife photography style with dark overlay and labels

## Slide 11 — Goosebumps vs Shivering
- **Visual:** Side-by-side comparison table/infographic
- **Left column:** Goosebumps — tiny skin muscles, raises hair, doesn't warm you, vestigial
- **Right column:** Shivering — skeletal muscles, rapid contracting, generates real heat
- **Text overlay:** "NOT the Same Thing!"
- **Style:** Clean comparison layout, blue/cold colour scheme

## Slide 12 — Hair Regeneration Discovery
- **Visual:** Microscope view of hair follicle with stem cells highlighted

- **Style:** Scientific/microscopy aesthetic, green/teal highlights
