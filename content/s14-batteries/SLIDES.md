# S14 — How Do Batteries Store Power? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A glowing AA battery and a smartphone side by side on a dark background, both radiating neon energy lines. A torch beam and a spinning clock float around them. Big question mark sparking with electricity in the centre.
- **Text overlay:** "How Do Batteries STORE Power?"
- **Style:** Electric blue and lime-green neon glow, bold condensed font for title, Katixo Shiksha logo top-left, subtle circuit-pattern background.

---
## Slide 2 — Myth Buster: Not Electricity
- **Visual:** Split screen. Left side: a crossed-out water tank labelled "Electricity?" with a red X. Right side: a battery cutaway glowing with green "chemical" molecules inside.
- **Text overlay:** "Batteries store CHEMICAL energy — not electricity!"
- **Style:** Red X stamp on the myth, green checkmark on the truth. High-contrast dark panel, comic-style burst behind the checkmark.

---
## Slide 3 — Meet the Parts
- **Visual:** Clean labelled cutaway of a single cell: anode (left, blue, minus sign), cathode (right, red, plus sign), electrolyte (gel texture between them), and a thin separator line down the middle. Each part with a neon callout label.
- **Text overlay:** "Anode (−) | Cathode (+) | Electrolyte | Separator"
- **Style:** Diagram style, glowing labels with thin connector lines, dark slate background, color-coded parts.

---
## Slide 4 — Redox Reaction
- **Visual:** Animated-style atoms at the anode shedding tiny glowing electrons (labelled e−), and atoms at the cathode catching them. Two arrows: "Oxidation" over anode, "Reduction" over cathode, merging into one word "REDOX".
- **Text overlay:** "Oxidation gives electrons. Reduction takes them. = REDOX"
- **Style:** Bright electron particles, motion blur arrows, periodic-table-inspired tile accents, neon orange highlights.

---
## Slide 5 — Electrons Flow Outside
- **Visual:** A full circuit loop: battery at the bottom, electrons (glowing dots) streaming OUT of the anode, through a wire, lighting up a bulb, and returning to the cathode. The separator blocks the inside path (shown with a dashed barrier and a small "no entry" icon).
- **Text overlay:** "Electron flow through the wire = ELECTRIC CURRENT"
- **Style:** Animated dotted current trail in lime-green, glowing bulb, dark background with a glowing loop path.

---
## Slide 6 — Ions Balance Inside
- **Visual:** Same cell cutaway showing TWO simultaneous flows: electrons moving in the external wire (outside) and ions drifting through the electrolyte (inside) in the opposite balancing direction. A circular arrow loop emphasises the complete circuit.
- **Text overlay:** "Outside: electrons. Inside: ions. One complete loop!"
- **Style:** Two distinct neon colors for electrons vs ions, big circular loop arrow, dark teal background.

---
## Slide 7 — Voltage = The Push
- **Visual:** A water-pressure analogy: a tall water tank with strong pressure jet next to a small cell labelled "1.5V (AA)" and a phone cell labelled "3.7V (Li-ion)". A glowing "PUSH" arrow on the electrons.
- **Text overlay:** "Voltage = the PUSH | AA = 1.5V | Phone = 3.7V"
- **Style:** Blue water gradient meets neon battery graphics, big bold voltage numbers, dynamic push arrows.

---
## Slide 8 — Primary vs Secondary
- **Visual:** Two columns. Left: "Primary — one-time" with zinc-carbon and alkaline batteries (remote, wall clock icons), a one-way arrow. Right: "Secondary — rechargeable" with a phone Li-ion battery and a circular recycle/charge arrow.
- **Text overlay:** "Primary = use once | Secondary = rechargeable"
- **Style:** Side-by-side comparison cards, red theme for primary, green theme for secondary, bold dividing line.

---
## Slide 9 — Charging Reverses It
- **Visual:** A phone plugged into a charger. A bold curved arrow showing the chemical reaction running BACKWARD, electrons and ions returning to their start positions. A small "reverse" loop icon.
- **Text overlay:** "Charging = running the reaction in REVERSE"
- **Style:** Glowing charge cable, reverse arrows in bright cyan, dark background with energy refill bar filling up.

---
## Slide 10 — History: Volta, 1800
- **Visual:** A vintage sepia illustration of Alessandro Volta beside his voltaic pile (stacked zinc and copper discs with brine-soaked cloth). A small glowing spark at the top showing steady current. Year "1800" in big numerals.
- **Text overlay:** "Alessandro Volta built the first battery — 1800"
- **Style:** Sepia-meets-neon contrast (old photo + modern glow), parchment texture panel, the unit "Volt" highlighted.

---
## Slide 11 — Inside Your Phone (Li-ion)
- **Visual:** Cutaway of a lithium-ion cell: graphite anode (layered grey), metal-oxide cathode (red), and glowing lithium ions shuttling back and forth with a double-headed arrow. A capacity badge reading "5000 mAh".
- **Text overlay:** "Li-ions shuttle: graphite anode ↔ metal-oxide cathode | Capacity = mAh"
- **Style:** Modern tech aesthetic, glowing lithium-ion dots, sleek phone silhouette, neon purple accents.

---
## Slide 12 — Quick Brain Check
- **Visual:** A glowing brain icon with a pause symbol. Three numbered question cards stacked on a dark quiz-show background. A big "PAUSE" button graphic.
- **Text overlay:** "Quick Brain Check! 1) Chemical or electrical? 2) Oxidation: which electrode? 3) Which is rechargeable?"
- **Style:** Quiz-show vibe, spotlight glow, bold numbered cards, yellow accent on the PAUSE prompt.

---
## Slide 13 — Answers
- **Visual:** Same three cards now flipped to reveal answers, each with a green checkmark. A small celebratory confetti burst.
- **Text overlay:** "1) Chemical 2) Anode (oxidation) 3) Secondary (rechargeable)"
- **Style:** Green checkmarks, confetti accents, reassuring dark-green panel, bold answer text.

---
## Slide 14 — Recap + Next Episode
- **Visual:** A quick visual recap strip (battery cutaway, electron flow, charge symbol) across the top. Below, a teaser card with a crying onion and watery eyes for the next episode. Subscribe button glowing at the bottom.
- **Text overlay:** "Recap done! NEXT: Why do onions make you cry? | Subscribe to Katixo Shiksha!"
- **Style:** Energetic recap montage, bright Subscribe button (red), teaser onion graphic with a teardrop sparkle, channel logo prominent.
