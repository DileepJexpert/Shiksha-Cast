# S15 — Why Do Onions Make You Cry? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A dramatic close-up of a person mid-cut over a chopping board, a single glowing teardrop frozen on their cheek, a halved onion glowing faintly with neon-green vapor rising from it. Background deep charcoal with subtle lab-grid texture.
- **Text overlay:** "Why Do Onions Make You CRY? 😭🧅"
- **Style:** Huge bold title in neon cyan with a green glow; small Katixo Shiksha logo top-left; teardrop has a soft blue glow. Cinematic vignette.

---
## Slide 2 — Onion's Underground Defense
- **Visual:** Cross-section of soil with an onion bulb growing underground, roots spreading out. Tiny cartoon pests (a worm, a beetle) approaching, repelled by a glowing shield around the onion. Sulfur atoms (yellow dots labelled S) being absorbed from soil into the bulb.
- **Text overlay:** "Onion = Underground Bulb with a Sulfur SHIELD 🛡️"
- **Style:** Earthy brown soil gradient at bottom, dark sky above. Neon-yellow sulfur dots, green protective shield glow. Bold white label text.

---
## Slide 3 — Two Chemicals, Kept Apart
- **Visual:** A magnified single onion cell shown as a rounded room split into two compartments. Left compartment labelled "Enzyme: Alliinase", right compartment labelled "Sulfur compounds: Sulfoxides (isoalliin)". A dotted wall keeps them separate. Calm, no reaction.
- **Text overlay:** "Inside the cell: 2 chemicals locked in SEPARATE rooms 🔒"
- **Style:** Diagrammatic, clean. Cell membrane glowing teal. Two compartments tinted differently (orange vs purple). Crisp callout labels with thin neon lines.

---
## Slide 4 — The Knife Breaks the Walls
- **Visual:** A sharp knife slicing through the onion, cell walls visibly shattering like breaking glass. The two compartments from Slide 3 now spilling and mixing together, swirling orange and purple merging.
- **Text overlay:** "CUT! → Cell walls break → chemicals MIX 💥"
- **Style:** Dynamic motion lines, shatter-glass effect on cell walls. Knife with metallic sheen. Bold red "CUT!" with impact burst. Dark dramatic background.

---
## Slide 5 — Step 1: Sulfenic Acid Forms
- **Visual:** A simple reaction-arrow diagram. Left: sulfur compound + alliinase enzyme (shown as a Pac-Man-style enzyme). Arrow pointing right to a wobbly, unstable molecule labelled "Sulfenic Acid". A small "unstable / intermediate" warning tag shaking.
- **Text overlay:** "Step 1: Enzyme makes SULFENIC ACID (unstable!) ⚗️"
- **Style:** Flat-style molecular diagram on dark background, neon-green reaction arrow, jittery glow on the unstable molecule to suggest instability.

---
## Slide 6 — Step 2: The Tear Gas Is Born
- **Visual:** The LFS enzyme (a glowing machine-like icon) converting sulfenic acid into a gas molecule. Floating green gas wisps rising upward labelled "syn-Propanethial-S-oxide". An arrow shows the gas drifting up toward a faint face silhouette above.
- **Text overlay:** "Step 2: LFS enzyme → tear gas: syn-Propanethial-S-oxide 💨"
- **Style:** Industrial-lab feel. LFS shown as a neon-orange converter. Gas wisps in translucent toxic-green. Upward motion arrows.

---
## Slide 7 — Gas Meets Eye Moisture
- **Visual:** Close-up of an eye with a thin shiny moisture layer on its surface. Green gas molecules hitting the wet surface and transforming into small droplets labelled "mild sulfuric acid". A red zoomed inset showing the cornea's nerve endings glowing in alarm.
- **Text overlay:** "Gas + eye water → mild SULFURIC ACID → stings nerves 😖"
- **Style:** Macro photo-realistic eye on dark backdrop. Green gas, red irritation glow on nerve endings. Inset circle with neon border. Reassuring small note: "(tiny amount — harmless!)".

---
## Slide 8 — Brain Triggers the Tears
- **Visual:** Side profile of a head showing a glowing nerve pathway from the eye to the brain. The brain lights up and sends a signal back to the lacrimal gland (above the eye), which releases tear droplets washing the eye.
- **Text overlay:** "Brain → ALERT! → Lacrimal glands make tears to wash it out 🧠💧"
- **Style:** Neon circuit-style nerve pathways (cyan signal going up, orange command coming back). Dark anatomical illustration. Glowing brain.

---
## Slide 9 — 3 Types of Tears
- **Visual:** Three labelled panels side by side: (1) Basal tears — eye staying moist; (2) Emotional tears — a happy/sad face; (3) Reflex tears — onion, smoke, dust icons with a highlighted glow. The reflex panel is spotlighted as "THIS ONE".
- **Text overlay:** "Onion tears = REFLEX tears (not emotional!) 🧅 = 🪟 wiper"
- **Style:** Three-card layout on dark background, each card with its own accent color. Reflex card glowing brightest with a neon ring. Clean icons.

---
## Slide 10 — Tips Part 1: Chill + Sharp Knife
- **Visual:** Split screen. Left: an onion inside a fridge with a frost glow and a "15 min" timer. Right: a sharp gleaming knife next to a blunt dull knife with a red cross over the blunt one. Small gas-level meters showing "less gas" for both tips.
- **Text overlay:** "Tip 1: Chill it 🧊 | Tip 2: Use a SHARP knife 🔪"
- **Style:** Two-panel comparison, icy-blue glow on fridge side, metallic shine on knife side. Green "less gas" meters. Bold numbered tip badges.

---
## Slide 11 — Tips Part 2: Water, Flame, Fan, Goggles
- **Visual:** A 4-icon grid: running tap over onion, a candle flame near the board, a fan blowing gas away, and a person wearing swim-style goggles while chopping. Each icon shows gas being neutralized or redirected.
- **Text overlay:** "More tips: 💧 water | 🔥 flame | 🌀 fan | 🥽 goggles!"
- **Style:** Clean 2×2 grid on dark background, each cell with a neon accent border and a small caption. Playful but informative. Numbered badges 3–6.

---
## Slide 12 — QUIZ: Quick Brain Check
- **Visual:** A glowing brain icon with a big PAUSE symbol. Three numbered question cards stacked. A countdown / hourglass element hinting "pause now".
- **Text overlay:** "🧠 QUICK BRAIN CHECK! ⏸️
1. Which 2 things stay separate in the cell?
2. Name the tear gas + the enzyme that makes it
3. What really stings — the gas or what it forms?"
- **Style:** Quiz-show aesthetic, bold yellow-on-dark question text, big neon PAUSE button. Energetic, suspenseful glow.

---
## Slide 13 — ANSWERS
- **Visual:** Same three cards now flipped to reveal answers with green checkmarks. Small molecule icons next to each answer for visual reinforcement.
- **Text overlay:** "✅ ANSWERS:
1. Alliinase enzyme + sulfoxides (isoalliin)
2. syn-Propanethial-S-oxide, made by LFS
3. Gas + eye water → mild sulfuric acid stings nerves"
- **Style:** Same quiz layout as Slide 12 but with green success glow, checkmarks, and a celebratory confetti accent. Clear readable answer text.

---
## Slide 14 — RECAP + Subscribe

Side note bubble: "Fun fact: scientists made a TEARLESS onion!" Bottom: a big Subscribe button with a bell icon and a comment bubble saying "What topic next?".
- **Text overlay:** "RECAP done! 🔬 Tearless onion exists! 👇 Comment your next topic | LIKE + SUBSCRIBE 🔔"
- **Style:** Bright, summary-style layout. Katixo Shiksha logo prominent. Neon Subscribe button in red, glowing bell. Friendly, upbeat closing vibe.
