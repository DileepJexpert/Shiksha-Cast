# S26 — Why Do You Get Brain Freeze? — Slide Design Brief

**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---

## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** Teen holding a melting ice cream cone, frozen mid-bite, holding forehead in pain; icy crystal effect radiating from head
- **Text overlay:** "BRAIN FREEZE?!" in bold electric-cyan with frost glow
- **Subtext:** "Sphenopalatine ganglioneuralgia"
- **Bottom bar:** Katixo Shiksha logo + "S26" badge
- **Style:** Dark navy background, neon cyan accents, dramatic ice-shard particles

## Slide 2 — Brain Has No Pain
- **Visual:** Cross-section of a brain with a big "NO ❌" symbol over tiny nerve icons; surgeon operating on awake patient inset
- **Text overlay:** "Your BRAIN Can't Feel Pain"
- **Key note:** "No pain receptors inside the brain"
- **Style:** Dark theme, glowing brain outline, magenta highlight on the myth-bust

## Slide 3 — The Palate Connection
- **Visual:** Side-profile head cut-away showing roof of mouth (palate) with internal carotid artery + anterior cerebral artery labeled near it
- **Text overlay:** "It All Starts at the Palate"
- **Callout:** "Cold touches the roof of mouth → arteries nearby"
- **Style:** Anatomical diagram, neon-orange artery lines on dark background

## Slide 4 — Squeeze & Expand
- **Visual:** Two-panel animation frames: artery narrowing (vasoconstriction) then widening (vasodilation), with rubber-band-snap motif
- **Text overlay:** "Arteries: Shrink → Expand → FAST"
- **Key terms:** "Vasoconstriction" + "Vasodilation"
- **Style:** Dark background, pulsing red/blue vessel graphics, motion arrows

## Slide 5 — Trigeminal Nerve Messenger
- **Visual:** Glowing nerve network across face highlighting the trigeminal nerve branching to mouth, eyes, forehead
- **Text overlay:** "The Messenger: Trigeminal Nerve"
- **Callout:** "Expanding arteries press the nerve → alarm signal"
- **Style:** Neon-yellow nerve glow tree on dark face silhouette

## Slide 6 — Referred Pain
- **Visual:** Diagram with cold source at mouth, dotted signal line traveling up, and pain "feeling" lighting up at forehead instead
- **Text overlay:** "Referred Pain: Felt in the WRONG Place"
- **Example tag:** "Like heart attack → left arm pain"
- **Style:** Dark theme, glowing red mismatch arrows, confusion-spark icon on brain

## Slide 7 — Not Everyone Gets It
- **Visual:** Crowd of people, ~35% glowing with frost icon, rest dim; small lab beaker + straw experiment inset
- **Text overlay:** "Only ~30–40% Get It Easily"
- **Key note:** "Migraine-prone people = more likely"
- **Style:** Infographic bars, neon-cyan highlights, dark background

## Slide 8 — How Long It Lasts
- **Visual:** Stopwatch showing 10–30 seconds with a calming green "harmless" shield badge
- **Text overlay:** "Lasts 10–30 Seconds. Harmless!"
- **Key note:** "Body's clever brain-protection system"
- **Style:** Dark background, green safety glow, ticking clock graphic

## Slide 9 — The Fix Tricks
- **Visual:** Three icon panels: tongue pressing palate, warm breath through hands, slow eating with front of mouth
- **Text overlay:** "3 Tricks to STOP It"
- **List:** "1. Tongue on palate  2. Warm breath  3. Eat slow & front"
- **Style:** Clean icon trio, neon-orange numbers, dark theme

## Slide 10 — Migraine Research Link
- **Visual:** Split screen — quick frost-spike (brain freeze, seconds) vs long red wave (migraine, hours); scientist studying both
- **Text overlay:** "A Safe Model to Study Migraine"
- **Key note:** "Easy to trigger AND stop on purpose"
- **Style:** Dark theme, dual timeline graphic, purple migraine wave

## Slide 11 — Summer Culprit
- **Visual:** Hot sun + sweating teen suddenly gulping a blue slushie; big temperature-contrast meter
- **Text overlay:** "Why Summer Makes It Worse"
- **Key note:** "Bigger hot–cold contrast = bigger shock"
- **Style:** Warm orange sky vs icy blue drink, dark border, contrast meter

- **Text overlay:** "3 Questions — PAUSE & Think!"

- **Text overlay:** "Answers!"
