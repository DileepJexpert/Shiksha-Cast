# S02 — How Does WiFi Actually Work? — Slide Design Brief

**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_015.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Tech/digital theme, circuit-board patterns, blue-cyan neon

---

## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** Invisible WiFi waves radiating from a router through walls, furniture, people (X-ray style cutaway)
- **Text overlay:** "How Does WiFi ACTUALLY Work?" in bold white + cyan glow
- **Bottom bar:** Katixo Shiksha logo + "S02" badge
- **Mood:** Futuristic, matrix/digital aesthetic

## Slide 2 — WiFi ≠ Internet
- **Visual:** Diagram showing: Global internet (undersea cables, servers) → ISP → Router → [WiFi zone: 10-20m wireless] → Phone
- **Highlight the WiFi zone** with a dotted circle — "this is WiFi"
- **Text overlay:** "Internet = Highway. WiFi = Your Driveway."
- **Style:** Infographic with distance labels, clean arrows

## Slide 3 — Radio Waves
- **Visual:** Electromagnetic spectrum bar showing radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, UV, X-rays, gamma
- **Highlight WiFi's position** — between radio and microwave (2.4 GHz / 5 GHz labeled)
- **Text overlay:** "WiFi = Radio Waves vibrating 2.4 BILLION times/sec"
- **Style:** Scientific spectrum diagram, rainbow gradient, WiFi frequency circled

## Slide 4 — Data Transfer Steps
- **Visual:** Numbered flowchart (4-5 steps):
  1. Phone encodes request → binary (1s and 0s)
  2. Binary → radio wave pattern
  3. Transmit via antenna
  4. Router receives → decodes → sends to internet
  5. Response travels back the same way
- **Style:** Flowchart with device icons, arrows, dark background

## Slide 5 — Modulation (Encoding Data)
- **Visual:** 3 wave diagrams showing:
  - Amplitude modulation (wave height changes)
  - Frequency modulation (wave spacing changes)  
  - Phase modulation (wave start point shifts)
- **Text overlay:** "QAM = All 3 at once → Billions of bits/sec"
- **Style:** Physics textbook style but modern — colored waves on dark background

## Slide 6 — 2.4 GHz vs 5 GHz
- **Visual:** Split comparison infographic:
  - Left (2.4 GHz): longer wave illustration, house floorplan showing full coverage, speed meter at "medium"
  - Right (5 GHz): shorter wave, floorplan showing limited coverage, speed meter at "fast"
- **Text overlay:** "Range vs Speed — Pick Your Trade-off"
- **Bottom note:** "WiFi 6E adds 6 GHz — even faster, even shorter range"

## Slide 7 — Multiplexing (Multiple Devices)
- **Visual:** Highway with multiple lanes, each lane labeled with a device (phone, laptop, TV, tablet)
- **Diagram:** Frequency band split into colored sub-channels, each assigned to a device
- **Text overlay:** "OFDMA — Every Device Gets Its Own Lane"
- **Style:** Highway metaphor combined with frequency diagram

## Slide 8 — Why WiFi Gets Slow
- **Visual:** 3-panel layout:
  - Panel 1: Signal strength dropping with distance (inverse square graph)
  - Panel 2: WiFi waves hitting wall → weakened on other side
  - Panel 3: Multiple routers overlapping = interference noise
- **Text overlay:** "Distance + Walls + Interference = Slow WiFi"
- **Style:** Problem-diagnosis aesthetic, red warning accents

## Slide 9 — Your Body Absorbs WiFi
- **Visual:** Split comparison — microwave oven heating food (2.45 GHz) vs WiFi router (2.4 GHz)
- **Human body outline** showing "60% water" with WiFi waves being absorbed
- **Text overlay:** "Same Frequency. 100,000× Less Power."
- **Style:** Mind-blown aesthetic, dramatic comparison layout

## Slide 10 — WiFi Security / Encryption
- **Visual:** Data packet being sent → encrypted (shown as scrambled text/lock icon) → received and decrypted
- **Diagram:** Router ↔ Device with "WPA3 Encryption" shield in between
- **Text overlay:** "Math Protects Your Data"
- **Style:** Cybersecurity aesthetic, green matrix-style text, lock icons

## Slide 11 — Myth Busters
- **Visual:** 3-panel "myth vs reality" layout:
  - ❌ "More bars = faster internet" → ✅ "Bars = signal strength, not speed"
  - ❌ "Expensive router = fast WiFi" → ✅ "Bottleneck is your ISP plan"
  - ❌ "WiFi causes cancer" → ✅ "Non-ionizing. Zero scientific evidence."
- **Style:** Bold myth/fact cards, red X and green checkmark icons

## Slide 12 — Future: WiFi 7 & Li-Fi
- **Visual:** Timeline/evolution graphic:
  - WiFi 4 → WiFi 5 → WiFi 6 → WiFi 7 (46 Gbps!) → Li-Fi (224 Gbps via light!)
- **Illustration:** LED bulb transmitting data as flickering light beams
- **Text overlay:** "WiFi 7: Download a Full Movie in <1 Second"
- **Style:** Futuristic, gradient timeline, glowing elements
