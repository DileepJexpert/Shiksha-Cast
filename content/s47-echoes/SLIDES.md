# S47 — Why Do We Hear Echoes? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** A lone teen standing at the edge of a misty mountain valley, hands cupped around the mouth shouting. Stylised neon sound-wave arcs ripple outward and a faded ghost-like duplicate of the same waves curves back. Dark teal-to-black gradient sky with stars.
- **Text overlay:** "Echo क्यों सुनाई देती है?" with a smaller kicker line "Katixo Shiksha"
- **Style:** Big bold neon-cyan title, glowing magenta accent on the word Echo, cinematic vignette, energetic comic-book feel.

---
## Slide 2 — What is Sound
- **Visual:** Split frame. Left: a cartoon mouth with concentric ripple rings of air particles being pushed. Right: a stone dropping into a pond making ripples, drawn as a direct analogy. A small inset of a planet in black space with a red NO-signal icon over a speaker.
- **Text overlay:** "Sound = कंपन (vibrations) | needs a MEDIUM"
- **Style:** Neon-green particle dots, dotted connector line between the two analogies, dark navy background.

---
## Slide 3 — Speed of Sound
- **Visual:** A speedometer-style gauge with the needle on 343. A racing sound-wave icon zooming across a track. Side-by-side mini icons: air, water (4x faster), iron (fastest) with arrow lengths showing relative speed. A lightning bolt flashing before a thunder symbol.
- **Text overlay:** "Sound speed in air ≈ 343 m/s"
- **Style:** Bold orange and cyan, motion-blur streaks, big number 343 glowing.

---
## Slide 4 — How Echo Forms (Reflection)
- **Visual:** A person shouting toward a tall cliff. Neon arrows show the sound wave travelling to the wall and bouncing straight back to the ears. A small mirror-and-face inset illustrates the light-reflection analogy.
- **Text overlay:** "Echo = your own sound REFLECTED back"
- **Style:** Two-tone arrows (cyan going, magenta returning), hard-edged surface texture on the cliff, dark slate background.

---
## Slide 5 — Why Not Always an Echo (0.1 s rule)
- **Visual:** A brain icon with a small clock showing 0.1 s and a fading sound trail. Two timelines: one where original and returning waves overlap (merged blob, X mark), one where they are clearly separated (two distinct pulses, tick mark).
- **Text overlay:** "Brain needs a 0.1 second gap to hear echo"
- **Style:** Neon-yellow highlights on the 0.1 s, comparison split with red X and green tick, dark charcoal background.

---
## Slide 6 — The 17 Metre Rule
- **Visual:** A diagram of a person and a wall with a double-headed arrow labelled ~17 m. Sound path drawn going to the wall and back (total ~34 m). A small calculation strip: 343 m/s × 0.1 s = ~34 m → half = ~17 m.
- **Text overlay:** "Wall must be ≥ 17 m away for a clear echo"
- **Style:** Blueprint-style measurement lines, glowing cyan distance marker, math strip in monospace neon, dark background.

---
## Slide 7 — Echo vs Reverberation
- **Visual:** Split comparison. Left: mountain with two clear separated HELLO bubbles (echo). Right: a temple or empty bathroom interior with many overlapping wavy lines blending into a smear (reverberation). A singing figure in the bathroom panel.
- **Text overlay:** "Echo = separate | Reverberation = blended gूंज"
- **Style:** Clear vs blurred contrast, neon-pink labels, dark moody interior tones.

---
## Slide 8 — Absorbing Sound
- **Visual:** A recording-studio wall covered in wedge foam panels. Sound waves hitting the foam and being soaked in (arrows shrinking into the foam). Icons of curtains, carpet and a sofa with absorb arrows.
- **Text overlay:** "Soft, porous materials ABSORB sound"
- **Style:** Textured foam pattern, muted purple acoustic vibe, neon-cyan absorb arrows fading out, dark background.

---
## Slide 9 — SONAR
- **Visual:** Cross-section of the ocean. A ship and a submarine sending a sound pulse downward; the pulse hits the seabed and a fish school, then returns as an echo arc. A timer icon measuring the round trip.
- **Text overlay:** "SONAR: echo measures depth & distance"
- **Style:** Deep-blue gradient water, sonar ping ring graphics, glowing green echo return, dark abyss at the bottom.

---
## Slide 10 — Echolocation in Nature
- **Visual:** A bat in flight emitting ultrasonic wave cones toward a moth, with the echo bouncing back. A dolphin underwater doing the same toward a fish. A small NO-EAR icon noting humans cannot hear ultrasonic.
- **Text overlay:** "Bats & dolphins SEE with echo (echolocation)"
- **Style:** Night-sky for bat panel, underwater teal for dolphin panel, neon ultrasonic cones, playful but accurate.

---
## Slide 11 — Recap of the Mechanism
- **Visual:** A clean 4-step horizontal flow with icons and arrows: 1) Mouth makes sound, 2) Waves travel through medium, 3) Hit a hard far surface and reflect, 4) Ear hears it after 0.1 s gap as echo. Distance tag ~17 m on step 3.
- **Text overlay:** "Sound → Travel → Reflect → Echo"
- **Style:** Numbered glowing nodes connected by neon line, infographic look, dark background.

---
