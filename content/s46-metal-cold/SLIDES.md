# S46 — Why Does Metal Feel Colder Than Wood? — Slide Design Brief
**Format:** 1920×1080 PNG | **Export:** `slides/slide_001.png` to `slide_014.png`
**Brand:** Katixo Shiksha | **Style:** Dark science theme, neon accents, bold text overlays

---
## Slide 1 — HOOK / Title
- **Visual:** Split frame on a dark background. Left: a glowing wooden chair tinted warm orange. Right: a metal chair tinted icy blue, frost particles around it. A confused teen reaching between both, big question mark glowing above.
- **Text overlay:** "METAL ठंडा क्यों? 🥶"
- **Style:** Huge neon cyan title, frost VFX on metal side, warm glow on wood side. Channel logo top-left.

---
## Slide 2 — Skin Measures Heat Flow, Not Temperature
- **Visual:** A stylised hand with an animated heat-flow arrow (orange) streaming out of the fingertips. A red "TEMPERATURE METER ❌" crossed out, a green "HEAT FLOW DETECTOR ✅" highlighted.
- **Text overlay:** "Skin = Heat Flow Detector, not Thermometer"
- **Style:** Neon arrows, bold contrast between the crossed-out and highlighted labels.

---
## Slide 3 — You Are Hotter Than the Room
- **Visual:** A body silhouette glowing red labelled "37°C". Metal and wood objects labelled "25°C". Arrows showing heat flowing FROM hand TO objects.
- **Text overlay:** "Heat flows: गरम → ठंडा"
- **Style:** Thermometer graphics, directional glowing arrows pointing outward from the hand.

---
## Slide 4 — Metal: The Heat Champion
- **Visual:** Close-up of a metal block with tiny glowing blue dots (free electrons) zipping fast, carrying orange heat sparks deep inside. A speed-meter pegged to MAX.
- **Text overlay:** "Metal = HIGH Thermal Conductivity ⚡"
- **Style:** Fast motion-blur electrons, neon speed gauge, dynamic energetic look.

---
## Slide 5 — Wood: The Insulator
- **Visual:** Cross-section of wood showing trapped air pockets (little bubbles). Heat sparks moving very slowly, almost stuck. A speed-meter near zero.
- **Text overlay:** "Wood = Insulator (हवा फंसी है)"
- **Style:** Soft warm tones, slow/blocked heat icons, air-bubble texture.

---
## Slide 6 — The Numbers
- **Visual:** Big bar chart. Metal bar towering at "50–400 W/m·K", wood bar tiny at "0.1–0.2 W/m·K". A "100–1000× faster!" callout.
- **Text overlay:** "Metal सैकड़ों गुना तेज़!"
- **Style:** Neon bar chart, glowing comparison numbers, dark grid background.

---
## Slide 7 — Summer Reversal
- **Visual:** A sunny scene: a hot metal car seat (radiating red, "OUCH") vs a wooden bench. Heat arrows now flowing FROM objects TO the seated person.
- **Text overlay:** "गर्मी में metal ज़्यादा गरम भी!"
- **Style:** Bright sun flare, heat-wave shimmer lines, reversed arrow direction.

---
## Slide 8 — The Blanket Proof
- **Visual:** A person under a woolly blanket, trapped body-heat shown as glowing warm aura held inside. Caption clarifying the blanket makes no heat itself.
- **Text overlay:** "कंबल गर्मी बनाता नहीं — रोकता है!"
- **Style:** Cozy warm glow, fibre/air-pocket texture in blanket.

---
## Slide 9 — Home Experiment
- **Visual:** A metal spoon and a wooden spoon side by side, a thermometer reading IDENTICAL on both, plus a cheek touching each with cold/normal reaction emojis.
- **Text overlay:** "Same temp, अलग एहसास! 🥄"
- **Style:** Clean tabletop scene, identical-temperature callout boxes, playful emoji reactions.

---
## Slide 10 — Where We Use It
- **Visual:** Three icons: a frying pan with a plastic handle, a wooden floor in a cold home, a computer metal heat-sink with fan.
- **Text overlay:** "Insulator vs Conductor = Engineering!"
- **Style:** Icon trio on dark panels, neon labels under each.

---
## Slide 11 — One-Line Summary
- **Visual:** A single clean diagram: hand + metal (fast arrows, "COLD") and hand + wood (slow arrows, "WARM"), tied together with a glowing bracket.
- **Text overlay:** "Skin feels HEAT FLOW, not temperature"
- **Style:** Minimal, high-contrast summary card, big readable text.
